📺 TV Energy Consumption in Australia

Screen technology, power usage & consumer buying insights.

Step 1: Who is the audience?

This story is designed for general consumers in Australia who want to buy a TV. They are not technical users, so the goal is to provide clear and practical advice using simple charts and plain language.

Step 2: What do they want to know?

Buyers mainly want to know which TV options use less electricity and which factors increase running costs. The most useful questions are:

  • Which screen technology types are common in the market?
  • Which technology tends to use less power on average?
  • Does choosing a larger screen significantly increase energy use?

Step 3: How are we presenting this information?

We use simple bar charts and a scatter plot so consumers can quickly compare technology types and understand the size-versus-power relationship. Supporting text under each chart explains what the visual means for real buying decisions.

Screen Technology Distribution

What this chart shows: How many TVs in the sample use LED, OLED, and LCD.
Why it matters: It shows which technologies are common and available for buyers.

Average Power Consumption by Technology

What this chart shows: Average power use in watts for each screen technology.
Why it matters: Lower average power can reduce electricity bills over time.

Screen Size vs Power Consumption

What this chart shows: The relationship between TV size and power use.
Why it matters: Larger TVs often use more energy, which affects long-term costs.

Conclusion

In this sample, OLED models generally use less power than similar alternatives, and bigger screens tend to consume more energy. Consumers can make smarter buying decisions by balancing size, technology, and running cost.
