Nuclear Energy

📖 History & Development

1942 first reactor Chicago Pile-1. 1951 first electricity (EBR-I). 1970s oil crisis boom. Chernobyl/Fukushima setbacks. New Gen III+ reactors safer. SMRs next frontier.

🔬 How It Works

Fission: U-235 absorbs neutron → splits → heat → steam → turbine. Control rods regulate. PWR/BWR/CANDU designs. Fuel lasts 18 months, waste vitrified.

✅ Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • Zero carbon emissions
  • High energy density
  • Baseload reliability
  • Low fuel cost

Challenges

  • High capital cost
  • Waste management
  • Public perception
  • Long construction

🚀 Future Outlook

SMRs (50-300MW) factory-built. Gen IV (molten salt/thorium) safer. Fusion demonstration 2030s. Nuclear essential for net-zero: 25% electricity target by 2050.
